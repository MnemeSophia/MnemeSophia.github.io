Sophie's "Dungeon Crawl" SRD

I have strong opinions about how D&D should be played. Rather than talk your ear off, these are my house rules for 0e. Why 0e? Because it begs to be house-ruled, and is the easiest edition to homebrew from.

Stats

Roll 3d6 and assign the results in order to Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma.

Stat Mod
3-6 -1
7-14 0
15-18 +1

STR: Apply to melee to-hit
DEX: Apply to ranged to-hit
CON: Apply to each HD when rolling HP
INT: Additional spell if caster
WIS: Apply to saving throws
CHA: Apply to reaction rolls and morale

Classes

The three classes are Fighter, Magic-User, and Thief. Pick one.

Fighter

Level Exp. HD
1 0 1d6+1
2 2,000 2d6+2
3 4,000 3d6+3
4 8,000 4d6+4
5 16,000 5d6+5
6 32,000 6d6+6

Fighters add their level to rolls to hit, and half their level to damage rolls. If they roll a 20 or higher to hit, they instantly slay their target. Fighters have advantage on saves against physical harm.

Magic-User

Level Exp. HD
1 0 1d6-1
2 2,500 2d6-2
3 5,000 3d6-3
4 10,000 4d6-4
5 20,000 5d6-5
6 40,000 6d6-6

Magic-Users may memorize two known spells per level every day, and cast one memorized spell per level every day. They may use magic-items, and create magic items, provided the necessary resources. Magic-Users have advantage on saves against spells. Magic-Users cannot both wear armor and cast spells.

Thief

Level Exp. HD
1 0 1d6
2 1,500 2d6
3 3,000 3d6
4 6,000 4d6
5 12,000 5d6
6 24,000 6d6

Thieves add half their level to rolls to-hit. If unencumbered and unobserved, a Thief may slip into the shadows and remain virtually invisible, as long as they are motionless. Thieves hit automatically when attacking from surprise, and do their HD in damage. With the proper tools, a thief can open most locks, even magical ones, in the space of a turn on a roll of 1-4 on a d6. For non-magical locks, they may make multiple attempts.
